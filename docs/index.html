How this strategy works — in plain English

It owns a rotating handful of the strongest large crypto coins — but only as much as the market's overall health allows, and it drops any coin that breaks its trend. The aim is to capture crypto's big up-moves while sidestepping the worst of its crashes.

1
Pick the strongest coins. Each week it ranks ~25 large Binance coins by risk-adjusted momentum (recent return per unit of choppiness, over 1, 3 and 6 months) and holds the top 4, equally weighted.
2
Invest only when the market is healthy — the safety valve. It measures how many coins are in uptrends ("breadth"). Weak breadth → it sits mostly in cash; strong breadth → fully invested (0 / 30 / 60 / 100 %). This gate is the single most important part: without it the return roughly halves and the worst loss doubles.
3
Buy confirmed uptrends, cut losers fast. A coin enters only if it is above its own rising trend line, and it is sold the next day if it breaks that line — strict on the way in, quick on the way out.
4
When it wins, and when it lags. It shines in broad altcoin rallies (2019, 2021) and cushions bear markets (2018, 2022, where the cash valve protects). It lags when Bitcoin alone leads (2020, 2023, 2024) — it is a diversifier, not a Bitcoin replacement.
Bottom line (2026-07-04 review + follow-up): a real but modest edge — on a like-for-like basis it beats simply holding Bitcoin in every period, and by the widest margin in the bad years, where its cash gate protects. Two catches keep it small: the edge is uncertain (a genuine chance it is no better than Bitcoin over a future run), and it is not a hedge — it moves with equities and falls with them in a sell-off. Treat it as a tiny return-seeking satellite sized for ~45% swings, not a diversifier and not a core holding.

How the strategy works

Six rules stacked in order. Each was tested in isolation; the gate ablation and the daily exit are the two single biggest contributors to the v3 numbers.

1Universe
25 USDT pairs on Binance. A coin is investable only when it has ≥90d of history AND trailing 30d ADV ≥ $25M. Failed coins (LUNA, FTT) are in the candidate pool, so there is no survivor bias within these 25 names — but the 25-name set is itself hindsight-selected (40 of 40 era-majors with real 2019–21 liquidity are absent), so every headline figure is an upper bound. See the Phase-2 survivorship audit.
2Breadth gate
% of investable universe above 50d MA, mapped to tiered gross exposure (0/30/60/100%). Tier graduation is decorative; the gate itself adds +0.55 Sharpe, +43 pp MaxDD improvement (re-measured under v3.1, ablation in scripts/research/diagnostics.py).
3Sizing
Composite momentum score over 30d, 90d, 180d risk-adjusted returns. Hold top-4 equal-weight when the gate is on. Adopted in v3.1 after walk-forward picked these in all seven re-fits. Fewer than 4 names may qualify, in which case the tier is spread across those that do — so v3.2 targets no single name above 34% of the book at each Monday rebalance, residual to cash. Without it the engine could put 100% in one coin (it did, 5 times). This is a rebalance-day target, not a standing constraint: weights drift with price between Mondays, so a realised weight can run higher (max 41.4% over the sample, against v3.1's 100%). The cap binds on 14 of 236 risk-on rebalances and is insurance, not an edge gain.
4Trend entry filter
A coin can only enter the top-N rank if close > own 50d MA AND the MA itself is rising. Strict — kills head-fakes.
5Trend exit (daily)
Asymmetric: close < own 50d MA triggers a forced sell at the next close (1-bar lag). Looser than entry so we do not whipsaw on normal pullbacks.
6Cadence and fees
Weekly Monday rebalance plus daily exit overrides. Signal at close T, traded close T+1. 10 bps per side (Binance VIP-0 spot).

What this strategy is not

2026-07-04 review + follow-up: a real but modest edge — small satellite, not a hedge

A pre-registered review, with a four-lens (quant / developer / CIO / portfolio) follow-up, found a genuine but modest active-crypto edge. (1) The edge is real on a like-for-like basis: stripping the same years from each, the strategy's risk-adjusted return beats Bitcoin and 60/40 in every window (full 1.35 vs 0.61; ex-2021 0.77 vs 0.55; ex-2020–21 0.62 vs 0.22) — the margin is widest in the bear years, where the breadth gate sits in cash while Bitcoin takes the full drawdown. The gate does real work; this is not a closet index. (2) But it is uncertain: an honest year-block bootstrap puts the Sharpe at [0.37, 2.14] — about a 1-in-8 chance it is no better than Bitcoin over a future run (the shorter-block [0.82, 1.92] understates this). (3) And it is not a diversifier: it moves with equities (beta ≈ 1 to the S&P 500), correlation rising in stress (0.21 → 0.34), −0.7% in equities' worst months — it amplifies risk-off, it does not hedge it. The 25-coin universe is also hindsight-selected, so the absolute headline is optimistic, and expect −45%+ drawdowns. The breadth gate is validated; concentration and a majors-only engine were tested and rejected. A tiny return-seeking satellite for active crypto exposure — not a hedge and not a core, and it overlaps crypto beta already present in a thematic equity sleeve. Full records: results/phase_d_followups.md, results/phase_b_review.md, results/survivorship_audit.md.

Not a strict BTC-improvement

Underperforms BTC in pure BTC-led bull years (2020, 2023, 2024). Outperforms in bears (2018, 2022) and breakout altcoin years (2019, 2021). It is a different risk profile, not a better one in every regime.

Lagging BTC since early 2024 (BTC-dominance regime)

Trailing ~17 months: strategy Sharpe 0.84 / CAGR +5.2 % vs BTC HODL's 1.77 / +23.7 %. The strategy is positive but materially behind a passive long-BTC. This is designed-for behaviour — diversified momentum loses to single-coin dominance when one coin leads. Worth understanding before deploying.

Capacity and execution costs

The 10 bps per-side fee is for Binance VIP-0 retail. At mid-size deployment ($1M+) realistic slippage on the smaller majors (LINK, NEAR, AVAX) is 30–80 bps. Re-measured fee sensitivity (v3.1, full sample): 10 bps → Sharpe 1.19, 25 bps → 1.15, 50 bps → 1.07, 100 bps → 0.91. OOS-only: 10 bps → 1.26, 50 bps → 1.13, 100 bps → 0.97. Capacity-aware Sharpe is the right number for real deployment — see scripts/research/diagnostics.py.

2018 bear is brutal

−43% CAGR in 2018 because the rolling liquidity universe had only 3 coins investable that year (BTC, BNB, ETH) and the strict trend filter whipsawed on false bounces. Known structural wound — candidate v4 change.

Out-of-sample is not pristine

The v3.1 parameter choice (top-4, momentum lookbacks 30 / 90 / 180) was selected via an expand-window walk-forward spanning 2020 → 2026 (see scripts/walk_forward_refit.py). The IS-best config matched the production default in all seven annual re-fits, which is unusually clean — but the post-2021 dataset has been looked at as part of the validation. The headline OOS Sharpe and the 90 % bootstrap CI [0.82, 1.92] are therefore post-selection and assume regime stationarity. Truly untouched out-of-sample begins 2027-01-01. Treat the post-2021 numbers as walk-forward-validated, not as a pristine held-out test.

What was rejected during research

Volatility-target overlay (re-tested under v3.1, 2026-06-01). IS Sharpe improved (1.32 vs 1.19 baseline) on the IS-best config (vol_target=40 %, lookback=15d). OOS Sharpe degraded (1.23 vs 1.45 baseline) — −0.22 OOS, the relationship between IS and OOS was inverted across the parameter grid. Honest IS-only protocol rejected the overlay. See scripts/research/vol_target_search.py.
